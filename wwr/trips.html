The Gray Fork, Utah

The Gray Fork River has something for everyone! It is the most popular introductory whitewater rafting river in Utah,yet it is also thrilling enough to satisfy experienced rafters. This classic river offers diverse scenery in the beautiful Sierra foothills; narrow rocky gorges, peaceful stretches of calm water, and over 20 Class II & III rapids! The river is divided into two sections: the Upper (Chili Bar Run) and the Lower (Gorge Run), providing All-Outdoors guests with an exclusive variety of trip options. Whatever experience you desire, there is an All-Outdoors South Fork trip for you!

The Gray Fork River is rated a Class II/III and is usually done in 6 days

The Gray Fork River has been recognized as a National Landmark. This designation is based on the uniqueness and rich history it hold.

The middle Fork Canyon, Arizona

The Middle Fork Canyon is perfect for the experienced rafter or for the more adventurous first timer! Participants should be reasonably athletic, in good physical condition, and are required to know how to swim. It is a true wilderness river...dazzling, clear water flowing through a backdrop of green, densely forested canyon walls, rising 2000' overhead.Side streams form natural pools and waterfalls to play in and enjoy on multi-day trips. Spectacular scenery combines with numerous Class III and Class IV rapids in this secluded canyon to create a California river experience that is hard to match.

CLASS I - X: The Middle Fork Canyon uses a unique river rating scale. The rapids in the Canyon are technically rated I - X ( 1 - 10) to accommodate such a wide variety of rapids and river variances. However, most people are familiar with the regular I - V river rating scale, so we often refer to both.
